ACCU POLICY

Accupolicy needed a brand that could do something difficult — feel trustworthy and authoritative to a CFO reviewing insurance options, while remaining warm and approachable for an individual buying their first policy. The challenge was to bridge that gap without watering down either end.

I led brand identity, logo construction, and UI mockups — translating competitive research and user insight into a visual system that works across both audiences.

Market Research
and Inspiration

I found some friction points in Lockton's website. The first was the overwhelming amount of options in their drop down menu which would scale away any average user — creates DECISION FATIGUE.

The large amounts of texts on each page creates a lot of COGNITIVE LOAD on the users because lets be real who wants to read that much unless you're paid to do it?

The imagery on the website felt corporate and non-relatable, which is great for CEO's and board meetings but NOT so much for the average user.

But that does not mean that they had a BAD WEBSITE. The website had great SYSTEMS ARCHITECTURE, as they encompassed a huge variety of insurance types and created user flows for related types of insurance.

The CLIENT TESTIMONIALS were in video format rather than the standard descriptions and star rating, which helped build a lot more trust in the brand, for all the targeted demographics.

The entire experience skews young and consumer-focused. The PINK COLOUR PALETTE and playful illustrations feel too casual for B2B buyers — a CFO reviewing corporate coverage would dismiss this immediately.

The simplified language works for renters insurance but falls apart for complex business policies. TOO BASIC FOR COMPLEX NEEDS — high-stakes decisions need detailed explanations.

Lacks enterprise case studies, industry certifications, or professional credibility markers. It feels YOUTH-ORIENTED, not enterprise-ready.

Illustrations and infographics break down complex insurance concepts. Makes an intimidating topic feel approachable — REDUCES ANXIETY and anyone can understand it.

Not just responsive — genuinely designed for mobile. Since most individuals buy insurance on phones, this strategic decision ACTUALLY CONVERTS better than the competition.

What I took from each

  • Clear information hierarchy

    Lockton's structured navigation showed me how to organise complex insurance categories without overwhelming users. I carried this into Accupolicy's sitemap.

  • Video-led social proof

    Their video testimonials built far more trust than star ratings. I pushed for the same approach — real faces, real stories, not generic copy.

  • Authority through restraint

    Dark palette, dense type, minimal decoration — it read as serious and credible. I borrowed the tone for the B2B-facing parts of the brand.

  • Design that reduces anxiety

    Lemonade proved that breaking down insurance into digestible visuals makes the product feel less intimidating. I used this for the individual-facing sections.

  • Mobile-first thinking

    Their mobile experience was genuinely considered — not just shrunk. I ensured every component in Accupolicy was designed for touch first, desktop second.

  • Approachable language

    Simplified copy for consumer flows. Where Lockton speaks to CFOs, Lemonade reminded me to also speak to someone buying insurance for the first time.

Logo Construction

Built on a grid.
Built to last.

The logomark is constructed on a precise geometric grid — every proportion intentional. The two squares represent the dual audiences Accupolicy serves: institutional and individual. Red for urgency and authority, blue for trust and clarity.

Colour Variations

One mark.
Every context.

The logo is approved in four configurations — full colour, reversed, single colour dark, and single colour light. This ensures it holds across all backgrounds without losing legibility or brand integrity.

Invalid Usage

What breaks
the brand.

Distortion, unapproved colour swaps, low-contrast placements, and crowded lockups all undermine the credibility the mark is meant to carry. These rules protect the visual equity built into every application.

Colour Palette

Two audiences.
One system.

Deep navy anchors the brand in authority — it's the boardroom. Signal red adds urgency and confidence. Together they form a palette that is bold without being aggressive, and professional without feeling cold.

Typography — Primary

Headlines that
command the room.

The primary typeface is used for all display and headline applications. Its clean, modern weight projects authority without feeling stiff — the right voice for a brand speaking to executives and individuals alike.

Typography — Secondary

Body copy that
actually gets read.

The secondary typeface handles all running copy, UI labels, captions, and data. Optimised for legibility at small sizes, it keeps the reading experience effortless — especially across mobile, where most individual users engage with the product.
